Nasi goreng

Description

Nasi Goreng is a beloved fried rice dish from Southeast Asia, particularly popular in Malaysia and Indonesia.
It features a savory combination of rice stir-fried with vegetables, protein (such as chicken, shrimp, or eggs), and various seasonings.
The dish is often topped with a fried egg and served with sambal (spicy chili paste), cucumber slices, and a sprinkling of crispy fried onions.
Nasi Goreng is versatile and can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of cooked jasmine rice (preferably cold, day-old rice works best)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 red chili (sliced, optional for spice)
  • 1 egg (fried)
  • 100g chicken breast or shrimp (optional, diced)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots)
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • Fried shallots or onions (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add garlic and onion, stir-frying until fragrant and softened.
  2. Add the chicken or shrimp, cooking until fully cooked through.
  3. Add the cold, day-old rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps.
  4. Stir in soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar, mixing well to coat the rice evenly.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables and continue stir-frying until they are tender.
  6. Push the rice to one side of the pan and crack an egg into the empty space. Scramble the egg and once cooked, mix it into the rice.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro, fried shallots, and serve with lime wedges and sambal on the side.
