Nasi Lemak

Description

Nasi Lemak is considered Malaysia’s national dish. It consists of fragrant coconut milk-infused rice served with a variety of accompaniments.
The rice is creamy and rich, complemented by crispy fried anchovies, peanuts, a hard-boiled egg, and the most essential component:
sambal, a spicy chili paste. Nasi Lemak is often eaten for breakfast, but it is beloved at all times of the day

Ingredients

  • 2 cups jasmine rice
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 pandan leaves (optional, for aroma)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup fried anchovies (ikan bilis)
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (cut in half)
  • 2 tablespoons sambal (spicy chili paste)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a pot, combine the rice, coconut milk, water, salt, and pandan leaves. Bring it to a gentle boil, then reduce to low heat and cook the rice for about 15–20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  3. While the rice cooks, prepare the accompaniments: fry the anchovies until crispy, roast the peanuts, and boil the eggs.
  4. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork and remove the pandan leaves.
  5. To serve, place a portion of the coconut rice on a plate. Add the fried anchovies, roasted peanuts, a hard-boiled egg, and a dollop of sambal on the side.
  6. Nasi Lemak can be enjoyed as is or with additional sides, such as cucumber slices, rendang, or fried chicken.
